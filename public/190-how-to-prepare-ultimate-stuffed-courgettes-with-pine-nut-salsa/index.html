The ingredients needed to make Stuffed courgettes with pine nut salsa:
  1. Make ready large courgette / zucchini, halve lengthways (500 g)
  2. Take garlic clove, crushed
  3. Prepare large egg, beaten
  4. Prepare parmesan cheese, finely grated

  7. Prepare large lemon: finely grate the zest to get 2 tsp, then juice to get 1 tbsp
  8. Make ready finely chopped oregano leaves, plus a few extra leaves to serve (5g)
  9. Get pine nuts, lightly toasted
  10. Take olive oil
  11. Prepare Salt

Steps to make Stuffed courgettes with pine nut salsa:
  1. Preheat the oven to 230ºC fan.
  2. Use a dessertspoon to hollow out the flesh of the courgettes and make them into the shape of canoes; don’t scrape them out completely, though - you want the sides to be about 1 cm thick and the courgettes should still hold their shape. 1. Transfer the flesh to a sieve, and squeeze and discard as much as liquid as you can; you should be left with about 100 g drained courgette flesh.
  3. Put this into a medium bowl and stir in garlic, egg, parmesan, breadcrumbs and 1/4 tsp of salt. Use your hands and crust the tomatoes well, then add these to the bowl of courgette mixture. Stir to combine and set aside. 1. In a separate bowl, mix the lemon zest, oregano and pine nuts. Stir half of this into the courgette mixture and set the rest aside for salsa.
  4. Place the hollowed courgettes on a medium baking tray or ovenproof dish, hollowed side facing up.
  5. Drizzle 1 tbsp or oil (in total) over the courgette and season 1/8 tsp of salt (in total).
  6. Spoon the courgette mixture back into the hollows and bake for 15 minutes, until the fillings is set and golden brown. 1. While the courgette are baking, make the salsa. Add the lemon juice, remaining 2 tbsp of oil and 1/8 tsp of salt to bowl of oregano and pine nuts. 1. Let the courgettes cool a little. Spoon over the salsa, sprinkle with oregano leaves and serve.
