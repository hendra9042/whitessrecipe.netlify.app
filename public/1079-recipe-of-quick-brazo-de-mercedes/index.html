The ingredients needed to make Brazo de Mercedes:
  1. Make ready meringue
  2. Get egg whites
  3. Prepare granulated sugar
  4. Take cream of tartar
  5. Make ready filling
  6. Prepare egg yolks
  7. Take condensed milk (14 oz / 397g)
  8. Make ready vanilla extract
  9. Take powdered sugar
Steps to make Brazo de Mercedes:
  1. Preheat oven to 350¬∞F.
  2. For the meringue, beat egg whites in a large mixing bowl using a hand mixer on mid setting. Add cream of tartar when eggs are a little bubbly.
  3. Gradually increase beat setting of hand mixer. If eggs starts to get foamy, gradually add sugar.
  4. Continue beating until soft peaks form.
  5. Lay a sheet of parchment paper on a 9"x5" baking tray. Brush parchment paper with a little oil. (You can dip paper towel on oil and spread it thinly on baking tray)
  6. Pour meringue mixture and spread evenly. (Like VERY even)
  7. Using a bread knife, pass through the meringue's surface (for aesthetic purposes, which I obviously, did not do ü§´)
  8. Bake for 25-30 mins at 350¬∞F or until surface starts to brown a little.
  9. On a separate mixing bowl, beat egg yolks. Then add condensed milk and mix well.
  10. Add vanilla extract.
  11. To make custard spread, heat egg yolk mixture on a cooking pot on low heat. Continue mixing until you have a pasty consistency. Set aside to cool.
  12. Once meringue is baked, sift powdered sugar on top. Lay a parchment paper.
  13. Turn the pan upsidedown. Spread custard filling THINLY (Which I didn't do as you can see from the picture above. LEARN from my mistake üòíü§£, Don't worry i'll remake it soon so don't shade me, okay? üòù).
  14. ** If the sides crusted, slice off using a bread knife***
  15. Roll the meringue (like a sushi) placing the end under to make sure the roll stays intact.
  16. Sift a little more powdered sugar making sure top is evenly covered from all that rolling.
  17. Serve and enjoy.
